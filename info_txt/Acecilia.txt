Antygomonas caeciliae Dal Zotto, 2015

Short description

Species withrelatively deepdorsal and ventral concavities along anterior margin of segment 1. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2 to 10. Well-developed cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 5, 8 and 9 (cuspidate spine on segment 9 more ventral than corresponding acicular spine).
MTS, LTS and LTAS present. LTS:LTAS ratio very high (< 110%), i.e., LTS are always longer than LTAS.

Key characters and similar species

Thespecies are distinguished from its congeners A. incomitataand A. oreasby differences in cuspidate spine patterns. It shows closer resemblance with A. gwenaeand A. paulae, but can be distinguished by its high LTS:LTAS ratio, where LTS typically will be at least 10% longer than LTAS.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromthe Mediterranean north-west coast of Italy (Ligurian Sea), and is found in coarse sand on 1 to 4 m depth.
