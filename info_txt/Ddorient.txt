Short description

Segment 1 consists of a closed cuticular ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates. Acicular spinesarepresent in middorsal series on segments 2 to 9, but those on segments 4 to 9 are alternatingly displaced to paradorsal positions. Lateral series withouttubes, but acicular spinesare presenton segments 6 to 9. LTSare present, but LTAS and MTS absent.

Key characters and similar species

are all characterised by the conspicuous, lateral displacement of dorsal spines. In species of Dracoderes, the spines are appear alternatingly in paradorsal positions. This displacement is even more extreme in Dracophyes, where the spines alternatingly would appear in subdorsal positions.

Species of Dracoderescan all be distinguished by their combinedpatterns of dorsal and lateralspines and tubes. Dracoderesorientalisdiffers from all congeners by lacking tubes in their lateral series. In addition, their dorsal spine on segment 9 is laterally displaced to paradorsal position, which differs from other congeners where the segment 9 spine would be back in middorsal position.
