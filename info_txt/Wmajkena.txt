Wollunquaderes majkenae Sø rensen & Thormar, 20 10

Short description

with segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2, and 4 through 10.
Cuspidate spines are present in the lateral series on segments 2, 4 to 6, and 8.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.

Key characters and similar species

The species of this monotypic genus are not recognised by a single, unique character trait. Instead, it should be recognised by its unique combination of characters. The species is most easily distinguished from othersits segment compositions combined with the presence oflateroventral acicular spines on segment 1. This combination of characters is only shared with Campyloderes vanhoeffeni/macquariae. However, the latter does not have cuspidate spines at all, whereas such spines are well-developed in W. majkenae.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described fromthe Coral Sea, southwest Pacific, well off Brisbane, Australiaand was found in coral sand mixed with shell gravel at 58 m depth.
