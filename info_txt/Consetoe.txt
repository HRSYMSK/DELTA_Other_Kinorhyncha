Short description

Species with segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, segments 2 to 10 of one tergal and two sternal plates, and segment 11 of one tergal and one sternal plate. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 10 in males and segments 1 to 9 in females. In thelateral seriesacicular spines are present on segments 1 to 9. Cuspidate spines are present in the lateral series on segments 2, 5, 8 and

9.
An additional pair of lateral cuspidate

spines are present on segment 4 in the megastigma -morphotype MTS and LTAS present. LTAS longer than MTS.

Adult dimorphism: Adult dimorphism is likely to occur in all species of Condyloderes

Key characters and similar species

Condyloderes setoensis appears to display intraspecific variationregarding absence or presence of lateral cuspidate spines on segment 4. Such spines are lacking in the original description of C. setoensis, which makes its cuspidate spine formula similar with C. flosfimbriatusand C. multispinosus.

Condyloderesflosfimbriatus and C. setoensiscan be distinguished by traits in the posterior segment margins.
In C. setoensisthe posterior segment marginshave thin, acicular fringe tips, whereas the fringe tips in C. flosfimbriatus areflower-likeand resemble little flosculi. In addition, C. setoensishas a broad indentation in the posterior segmentmargin of segment 1, spanning between the ventromedial positions, and no fringe-like structures are present all along thisindentation. Conversely, C. flosfimbriatus has no, or only avery weak indentation, and minute acicular fringe-tips arepresent along the ventral side of segment 1.

C. setoensisand C. multispinosus is more challenging as the differences are restricted to presence or absence of sensory spots.
C. multispinosusdiffers from C. setoensisby the lack of a ventromedial sensory spot on segment 5, as well as by the existence of a sensory spot laterodorsallyon segment 1 and paradorsally on segment 7.

The other morphotype, originally described as C. megastigma, is easily distinguished from the two species mentioned above by the presence of lateral cuspidate spines on segment 4. However, this pair of spines makes its spine pattern similar to C. paradoxa. Thus, also C. paradoxa and the megastigma -morphotype need to be distinguished by sensory spot patterns.
Condyloderes paradoxus differs from the  megastigma-morphotype by its presenceof a sensory spot laterodorsally onsegment 1, paradorsally on segment 7, and sublaterally on segment 8.
