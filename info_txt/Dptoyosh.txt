Short description

Segment 1 consists of a closed cuticular ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates. Acicular spinesarepresent in dorsal series on segments 1 to 9, butonly the spine on segment 1 is middorsal.
From segment 2, the dorsal spines are alternatingly, laterally displaced, to paradorsal positions on segment 2 and 3, and even more extreme to subdorsal positions on segments 4 to 9. Long, flexible ventrolateral spines are present on segment 1, and more regular looking acicular spines in lateral series on segments 2 to 9. Lateral tubes are present on segments 2 to 9. Lateroventral glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 10.
LTS arepresent, but LTAS and MTS absent.

species

are all characterised by the conspicuous, lateral displacement of dorsal spines. In species of Dracoderes, the spines are appear alternatingly in paradorsal positions. This displacement is even more extreme in Dracophyes, where the spines alternatingly would appear in subdorsal positions.

Species of Dracophyesare distinguished by theposition of tubes in the lateralseries. Dracophyes toyoshioaeis easily distinguished from its two congeners, by having tubes in the lateral series on all segments, from segment 2 to 9. On the opposite, D. cepedaihas only tubes on segments 2, 5 and 8, and D. chaac only on segments 2 and 5.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from 697 m, at a locality west of Okinawa, Japan.
