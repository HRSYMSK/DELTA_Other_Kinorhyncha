Short description

Conspicuouslyrectangular trunk, with flattened ventral side and arched dorsal side. Segments 1 and 11 composed of a tergal and a broad, undifferentiatedsternal plate that articulate along the lateral margin of the trunk; segments 2 to 10 composed of one tergal and two sternal plates.
Segments 1 to 10 without acicular spines, buthave instead long, projecting, spinose cuticular processes present along the middorsal and lateroventral lines (but note that themiddorsal process on segment 10 is replaced by a flexible, articulating spine in males).

Segment 11 with middorsal rigid spine in females, and a flexible spine in males. Lateral terminal spines present.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Mixtophyesarebe distinguished by their stout rectangulartrunks, with a first segment composed of a tergal and broad, undifferentiated sternal plate, combined with thepresence of lateral terminal spines.
The lateral terminal spines make it easy to distinguish Mixtophyesfrom the otherwise similar looking genus Neocentrophyes.

The two known species of Mixtophyes, M. abyssalisand M. toothlessiare distinguished by their distributions of setae. M. toothlessigenerally shows a much higher number of setae, with series of paradorsal, paralateral and ventrolateral setae on segments 2 to 9, whereas M. abyssalisonly has paradorsal setae on segments 7 and 9, paralateralsetae on segments 2 and 7 to 9, andventrolateralsetaeon segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

This deep-sea speciesis only known from the Guinea Basin in the tropical East Atlantic, where it has been collected on depth from 5.136 to 5.174 m.
