Short description

h segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, segments 2 to 10 of one tergal and two sternal plates, and segment 11 of one tergal and one sternal plate. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 10, and lateral series on segments 1 to 10. Cuspidate spines are presentas unpaired ones inparadorsal positions on segments 3 and 7, in the lateral series on segments 2, 7 and 9, and ventrolaterally on sternal plates of segments 5 and 8.
MTS and LTAS present. LTAS always considerably longer than MTS.

Adult dimorphism: Adult dimorphism is likely to occur in all species of Condyloderes. It is still not fully understood for all species, but itappears that in particular occurrenceof acicular spines on segment 10 might differ between stages and sexes, thus researchers should be cautious about relying too much on segment 10 morphology during identification.

Key characters and similar species

All species of Condyloderes are distinguished by the positions of cuspidate spines.
C. agnetisis one of only three species with cuspidate spines on the dorsal side (other two are C. shirleyi and C. storchi). The species is easily distinguished from all others by presence of paradorsal cuspidate spines on segment 3. It can furthermore be distinguished from C. shirleyi and C. storchiby its lack of mid- or paradorsal cuspidate spines on segment 5.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from the Mediterranean, off the northwest coast of Sicily, Italy, but has also beenrecorded from Rovinj in the Adriatic Sea.
