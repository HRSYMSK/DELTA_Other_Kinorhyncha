Short description

Species with relativelyweakdorsal and ventral concavities along anterior margin of segment 1. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2 to 10. Well-developed cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 5, 8 and 9.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.
LTS:LTAS rationearly equal, or LTAS slightly longer than LTS.

Key characters and similar species

The species are distinguished from its congeners A. incomitata and A. oreasby differences in cuspidate spine patterns. It shows closer resemblance with A. gwenaeand A. caeciliae, but isdistinguished by its LTSand LTASbeing nearly equal in length. Opposite to this, in A. gwenaethe LTAS are nearly twice as long as its LTS, whereas LTS are longer than LTAS in A. caeciliae.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromthe Northwest Atlantic, 4-5 milesoff Fort Pierce, Florida, and is found inclean shell hash on 1 5 mdepth.
In addition, potentially conspecific specimens were reported from coarse, sandy sediments at 1-3 m depth near Bocas del Toro, Panama, and from coral sand on 3 m depth at Bermuda. The latter record was originally reported as Antygomonascf. oreas.
