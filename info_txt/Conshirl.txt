Short description

h segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, segments 2 to 10 of one tergal and two sternal plates, and segment 11 of one tergal and one sternal plate. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 1 1, and lateral series on segments 1 to 10. Cuspidate spines are present in as unpaired ones inmiddorsal positions on segments 5 and 7, andin the lateral series on segments 2 (two pairs!), 5 to 9.
MTS and LTAS present. LTAS always considerably longer than MTS.

Adult dimorphism: Adult dimorphism is likely to occur in all species of Condyloderes. It is still not fullyunderstood for all species, but it appears that in particularoccurrenceof acicular spines on segment 10 might differ between stages and sexes, thus researchers should be cautious about relying too much on segment 10 morphology during identification.

Key characters and similar species

All species of Condyloderesare distinguished by the positions of cuspidate spines. C.
shirleyiis one of only three species with cuspidate spines on the dorsal side (other two are C. agnetis

and C. storchi). The species is easily distinguished from all others byits double pairs of lateralcuspidate spines on segment 2. It is furthermore the only Condylodereswith lateral cuspidate spines on segment 6.

Distribution and habitat

Glacier Bay, Alaska, not too far from Juneau.
