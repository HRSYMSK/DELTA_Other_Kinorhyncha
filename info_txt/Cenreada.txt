Key characters and similar species

The species show great resemblance with C.
drakei, but can be distinguished from other congeners by its lack of tubes on segment 7 (distinguishes it from C. barbanigraand C. bonnyae), and lateral acicular spines on segment 8 and 9, which might differ in length, but do not show any particular differences in thickness and robustness (distinguishes it from C. impurus and C. spinosus). The species is most easily distinguished from C. drakeiby the relative differences in length between LTS and LTAS. In C. drakeithe LTS:LTAS proportions are 44-61%, whereas the difference is smaller in C. readae, which has the LTS:LTAS proportions of 63-78%.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from Florida, but has been recorded at numerous localities, from the southern part of the Gulf of Mexico, along the US East Coast to Newfoundland.
