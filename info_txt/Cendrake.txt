Short description

with segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 9, and on segment 11.
Males with additional acicular (stage 1) or crenulated (stage 2) middorsal spine on segment 10.
Long flexible ventrolateral acicular spines on segment 1. Tubes present inlateral series on segments 2 and 5.

Lateroventral acicular spines present on segments 8 and 9; the two pairs of spines do not show any conspicuous differences in shape or robustness.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.

Key characters and similar species

The species show great resemblance with C. readae, but can be distinguished from other congeners by its lack of tubes on segment 7 (distinguishes it from C. barbanigra and C. bonnyae), and lateral acicular spines on segment 8 and 9, which might differ in length, but do not show any particular differences in thickness and robustness (distinguishes it from C.
impurusand C.
spinosus). The species is most easily distinguished from C. readaeby the relative differences in length between LTS and LTAS. In C. drakeithe LTS:LTAS proportions are 44-61%, whereas the difference is smaller in C. readae, which hasthe LTS:LTAS proportions of 63- 78%.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from the Bermuda, and hasalso been recorded from the Gulf of Mexico.
