Short description

Long, cylindrical specimens, with a very thin cuticle, which makes it difficult to visualise plate junctions and articulations. Segments 1 and 2 consist of closed rings, and segments 3 to 11 of single plates with midventral articulations.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 4, 6, and 8 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 8 and 9.
Cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, and 4 to 9.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.
MTS always much longer that lateral spines.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Zelinkaderesare most easily distinguished from its congeners by the pattern of cuspidate spines in the lateral series, and each species has its own, unique pattern.
Z. submersusisthe Zelinkaderesspecies with cuspidate spines on most segments. No other congener has cuspidate spines continuously from segment 4 to 9, and especially the presence of cuspidate spines on segment 7 is unique within the genus.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from the North Sea, 25 km north of the Danish city Hanstholm on the west coast of Jutland, fromheterogeneous sand at 3 0 m depth.
The species has not been recorded elsewhere.
