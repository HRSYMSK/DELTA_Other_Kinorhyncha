Lateroventral acicular spines present on segments 8 and 9; the spineon segment 9 is considerably longer and stronger than the one on segment 8.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.

Key characters and similar species

Identification using positions of tubes shouldbe done with caution for this species, because appearance of tubesshows dimorphism, and

Distribution and habitat

The species was described fromsandy mud at 9 m depth near Singapore. The species has never been recorded elsewhere.
