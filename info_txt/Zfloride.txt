Short description

Long, cylindrical specimens, with a very thin cuticle, which makes it difficult to visualise plate junctions and articulations. Segments 1 and 2 consist of closed rings, and segments 3 to 11 of single plates with midventral articulations.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 4, 6, and 8 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2, 8, and 9.
Cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 5, 8 and 9.

MTS, LTS and LTAS present. MTS always much longer that lateral spines.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Zelinkaderes

are most easily distinguished from its congeners by the pattern of cuspidate spines in the lateral series, and each species has its own, unique pattern.
Z. floridensisisuncommon by showing cuspidate spines on only three segments. It is the only species of Zelinkaderes without cuspidate spines on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from the Northwest Atlantic, 20 milesoff Fort Pierce, Florida, from muddy sand at 140 m depth.
