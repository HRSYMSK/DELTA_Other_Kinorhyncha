Short description

Stout, rectangular trunk, with flattened ventral side and arched dorsal side. Segments 1 and 11 composed of a tergal and a broad, undifferentiatedsternal plate that articulate along the lateral margin of the trunk; segments 2 to 10 composed of one tergal and twosternal plates. Segments 1 to 10 without acicular spines, but have insteadprojecting, spinose cuticular processes present along the middorsal and lateroventral lines. Segment 11 with middorsal, rigid, articulated, spinous appendage.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Neocentrophyes

can be distinguished by their stout trunks, with a first segment composed of a tergal and broad, undifferentiated sternal plate. This, combined with the lack of lateral terminal spines makes it easy to distinguish the genus.

differentiation is more challenging, because only male morphology is known for N. intermedius, whereas only female morphology is known for N. satyai. Thus, the two species can be distinguished by their sexual characters, i.e., middorsal, articulating flexible spines on segment 10 and penile spines in N. intermedius, and middorsal spinous process on segment 10 and no penile spines in N. satyai. It is not unlikely that N. intermedius and N. satyai actually represent the two sexes of the same species.

Distribution and habitat

The speciesis only known from its type locality in the Bay of Bengal, 10 km east of Visakhapatnam in India. It was collected at 40 m depth from brow, sandy mud.
