Short description

h segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, segments 2 to 10 of one tergal and two sternal plates, and segment 11 of one tergal and one sternal plate. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 10, and lateral series on segments 1 to 10. Cuspidate spines are present in the lateral series on segment 9, andon the sternal plates, in ventrolateral (nearly ventromedial)
positions on segment 5, andventrolaterally on segment 8.
MTS and LTAS present. LTAS always considerably longer than MTS.

Adult dimorphism: Adult dimorphism is likely to occur in all species of Condyloderes. It is still not fully understood for all species, but it appears thatin particular occurrenceof acicular spines on segment 10 might differ between stages and sexes, thus researchers should be cautious about relying too much on segment 10 morphology during identification.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognised by its shortage of cuspidate spines, as it is the only species with lateral cuspidate spines on segment 9 only.

Distribution and habitat

was describedfrom the Mediterranean north-west coast of Italy (Ligurian Sea).
