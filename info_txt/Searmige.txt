Semnoderes armiger Zelinka,

Short description

Segment 1 consists of a closed cuticular ring, with deep middorsal and midventral, wedge-shaped incisions. Closing of the head opening follows a lateralcontraction, rather than the more common radial closing. Middorsal and midventral placids elongate and narrow. Segments 2 to 11 composed of one tergal and two sternal plates.

Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2 to 10. Well-developed cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 5, 6, 8 and 9.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.

Key characters and similar species

The species is distinguishedfrom S. luscaand S. pacificby its presence of cuspidate spines on segment 6, and from S. ponticusby presence of cuspidate spines on segment 2, but absence on segment 4.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from the Northern Adriatic Sea, off Rovinj, Croatia, but has been recorded at numerous localities throughout the Black Sea, Mediterranean, West European Atlantic Ocean. Besides the published records, the species is also known from Norway (Bergen), Faroe Islands (Kalbak Fjord), and Denmark (Helsingør).
