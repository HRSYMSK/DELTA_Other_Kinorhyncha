Short description

Long, cylindrical specimens, with a very thin cuticle, which makes it difficult to visualise plate junctions and articulations. Segments 1 and 2 consist of closed rings, and segments 3 to 11 of single plates with midventral articulations.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 4, 6, and 8 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2, 8, and 9.
Cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 4 to 6, 8 and 9.

MTS, LTS and LTAS present. MTS always much longer that lateral spines.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Zelinkaderesare most easily distinguished from its congeners by the pattern of cuspidate spines in the lateral series, and each species has its own, unique pattern. The lateral cuspidate spine pattern in Z. brightae is unusual because it has cuspidate spines on segment 4. This is only shared with Z. submersus, but this species also has, opposite to Z. brightae, also cuspidate spines on segment 7.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from the Northwest Atlantic, 6 milesoff Fort Pierce, Florida, and has subsequentlybeen collected in Mobile Bay, Alabama, Gulf of Mexico.
