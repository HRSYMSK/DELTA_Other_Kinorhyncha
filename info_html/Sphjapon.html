Sphjapon

Sphenoderesjaponicus Adrianov & Maiorova,

Shortdescription

with acicular lateroventral spines on trunk segments 3– 9; additional pair of cuspidate spines in lateroventral position on trunk segment 5; trunk segment 9 with a pair of large ventromedial papillae in females; trunk segment 10 with a pair of acicular (in female) or crenulated (in male) spines in laterodorsal/midlateral position; trunk segment 11 with very long midterminal spine (93– 95 % of trunk length), pair of lateral terminal spines and additional

pair of long acicular spines in laterodorsal positions.
Midventral placid droplet-shaped, terminating anteriorly into blunt tip.

Key characters and similar species

has cuspidate spines on segment 5 only, whicheasily distinguishes it from S. indicus (cuspidate spines on segments 5, 8 and 9), S. neptunus and S. poseidon (both with cuspidate spines on segments 5 and 9).
It shows greatest resemblance to S.
aspidochelone, and the two species share the same spine formulas.
They can, however, be distinguished by the more pointed (droplet-shaped) midventral placid in S. japonicus, that terminates into a blunt tip. Oppositely, the midventral placid in S. aspidocheloneterminates anteriorly into a transverse margin.

Distribution and habitat

Japan, off the southeast coast of Russia, at 5 15 to 1.530 m depth, and Aleutian Trench at 4.300 to 6.
550 m depth.
