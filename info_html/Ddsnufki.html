Short description

Segment 1 consists of a closed cuticular ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates. Acicular spinesare present in middorsal series on segments 2 to 9, but those on segments 3 to 8 are alternatingly displaced to paradorsal positions.
Lateral series with tubes on segments 2, 5, and 8, and acicular spines on segments 6 to 9.
LTS arepresent, but LTAS and MTS absent.

Key characters and similar species

are all characterised by the conspicuous, lateral displacement of dorsal spines. In species of Dracoderes, the spines are appear alternatingly in paradorsal positions. This displacement is even more extreme in Dracophyes, where the spines alternatingly would appear in subdorsal positions.

Species of Dracoderescan all be distinguished by their combined patterns of dorsal and lateral spines and tubes. Dracoderes snufkini does not show any single, unique characters, but is distinguished by this combined spine and tube pattern. It shows closest resemblance to D. abei, but is distinguished from this species by its absence of lateral tubeson segment 10.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from muddy sand at 30 m depth near Okinawa, Japan. It has also been recorded from Iriomote Island, southwest of Okinawa.
