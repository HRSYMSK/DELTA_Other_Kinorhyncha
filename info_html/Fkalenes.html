Franciscideres kalenesos

Franciscideres kalenesos Dal Zotto et al.,

Short description

Conspicuously elongate, slender and cylindrical species. The trunk cuticle is extremely thin and flexible, and tegumental plate junctions can be difficult to visualise.
Placids arecompletely reduced, and the neck region forms a closed ring that resembles an additional segment anterior to the regular trunk segments.
Segments 1, 2 and 11 are forming closed rings, whereas segments 3 to 10 are composed of single plates with midventral articulations.

Acicular spines present inmiddorsal series on segments 1 to 11 ( spines might be alternating slightly to paradorsal positions). A midterminal spine is absent, but the strong middorsal spine on segment 11 could be confused with a midterminal spine. Inthe lateral series, acicular spines are presenton segments 3 to 10, and tubes on segments 2, 5 and 8.
LTS and LTAS are present.

Key characters and similar species

Franciscideres kalenesos can only be confused with species of Cateria and Gracilideres. However, the segment composition differs between the three genera, and only F. kalenesos hassegments 1, 2 and 11 forming closed rings, combined with segments 3 to 10 composed of single plates with midventral articulations. Out of these species, F. kalenesosis furthermore the only one with middorsal spines on all segments.

Distribution and habitat

Thisspecies was described from subtidal, sandy sediments at the island, Ilhabela, near São Sebastiã o, Brazil. It has subsequently been sampled more frequently on different high-energy beach around Guaratuba, Brazil, where it occurs in the surf zone at about 1 m depth.
