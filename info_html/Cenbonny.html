In addition, males may have a crenulated middorsal spine on segment 10, but adult dimorphism is not fully understood for this species.

Long flexible ventrolateral acicular spines on segment 1. Tubes present inlateral series on segments 2, 5, 7 and 8.
Lateroventral acicular spines present on segments 8 and 9; the two pairs of spines do not show any conspicuous differences in shape or robustness. MTS, LTS and LTAS present.

Key characters and similar species

The species is most easily distinguished by the presence of paired lateroventral tubes on segment 7 – this character is only shared with C. barbanigra. However, in addition, C. bonnyae also has lateroventral tubes on segment 8, which is unique within the genus.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from the Bermuda, and has not been reported outside this island.
