Short description

Segment 1 consists of a closed cuticular ring, with deep middorsal and midventral, wedge-shaped incisions. Closing of the head opening follows a lateralcontraction, rather than the more common radial closing. Middorsaland midventral placids elongate and narrow. Segments 2 to 11 composed of one tergal and two sternal plates.

Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2 to 10. Well-developed cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 4 to 6, and 8 to 9.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present; LTAS same length, or slightly longer than LTS.

Key characters and similar species

The species iseasily distinguishedfromits congeners by its unique pattern of cuspidate spines, i.e., S. ponticusis the only Semnoderes species withoutcuspidate spines on segment 2, but with cuspidate spines present on segment 4.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from the Black Sea, near Constanta, Romania, and has not been recorded since its discovery.
