Short description

uter oral styles partially fused with mouth cone in proximal ends.
Neck with only 14 placids (opposite to the more common 16 placids.

Species with segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 10, andin lateral series on segments 3 to 9 (plus laterodorsal spines on segment 10 in males); lateral accessory spines present on segment 5. Cuspidate spines are not present.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present. LTASalways considerably longer than both MTS and LTS.

Not reported, but should be considered as existing.

Key characters and similar species

The outer oral styles being partly fused with the mouth cone, distinguishes R. casarrubiosiand its only congener, R. iejimaensis, from all other kinorhynchs. The two species are distinguished from each other by theoccurrence of middorsal spine on segment 1 and lateroventral spines on segment 3 (present in R. casarrubiosionly). Differences regarding occurrence of spines is also reportedfor segment 10, but these differences could potentially be subject to adult dimorphism, and should be used with some caution in species identification.

Distribution and habitat

was described from localities in the Mozambique Channel, close to the northwest coast of Madagascar, from muddy sediments at 754 to 757 m depth.
