Short description

Segment 1 consists of a closed cuticular ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates. Acicular spinesarepresent in middorsal series on segments 2 to 9, but those on segments 3 to 8 are alternatingly displaced to paradorsal positions.
Tubes present inseries on segments 2, 5, and 10, and in laterodorsal positions on segment 8. Acicular spines in lateral series presenton segments 3 to 9.
LTS arepresent, but LTAS and MTS absent.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Dracoderescan all be distinguished by their combinedpatterns of dorsal and lateralspines and tubes. Dracoderesspyro has several uniquetraits, and is easy to distinguish from its congeners. Unique traits include the presence of acicular lateroventral spines on segments 3 and 4, and the laterodorsal displacement of tubes on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat

This Caribbean specieswas described frommuddy sediments at 3-5 m depth near Haiti. It has been collected at several localities around Haitiand the Dominican Republic, and also from Puerto Rico.
