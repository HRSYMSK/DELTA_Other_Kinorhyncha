Short description

Long, cylindrical specimens, with a very thin cuticle, which makes it difficult to visualise plate junctions and articulations. Segments 1 and 2 consist of closed rings, and segments 3 to 11 of single plates with midventral articulations.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 4, 6, and 8 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2 (very small), 8, and 9.
Cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 5, 8 and 9.

MTS, LTS and LTAS present. MTS always much longer that lateral spines.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Zelinkaderesare usuallydistinguished from theircongeners by the pattern of cuspidate spines. However, the cuspidate spine patterns in Z. klepali and Z. yong are identical. These two species are distinguished by the presence (in Z. klepali) or absence ( Z. yong) of acicular lateral spines on segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from the Red Sea, close to Tubya al-Bayda, Egypt, from coralsand at 0,5-1,5 m depth. A specimen with an apparently very similar morphology was reported from Mobile Bay in the Gulf of Mexico, but due to the geographic distance from the type locality, conspecifity is unlikely.
