Ryuguderes iejimaensis

Ryuguderes iejimaensis
Yamasaki, 2016

Short description

uter oral stylespartially fused with mouth cone in proximal ends.
Neck with only 14 placids (opposite to the more common 16 placids.

Species with segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, and segments 2 to 1 1 of one tergal and two sternal plates.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 2 to 9, ( 2 to 10 in males), andinlateral series on segments 4 to 9 (plus laterodorsal spines on segment 10 in males); lateral accessory spines present on segment 5.

Cuspidate spines are notpresent.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.

LTAS always considerably longer thanboth MTSand LTS.

Adult dimorphism:
Not reported, but should be considered as existing.

Key characters and similar species

The outer oral styles being partly fused with the mouth cone, distinguishes R. iejimaensis and its only congener, R. casarrubiosi, fromall other kinorhynchs. The two species are distinguished from each other by the occurrenceof middorsal spine on segment 1 and lateroventral spines on segment 3 (present in R. casarrubiosionly). Differences regarding occurrence of spines is also reportedfor segment 10, but these differences could potentially be subject to adult dimorphism, and should be used with some caution in species identification.

Distribution and habitat

was described from a submarine cave near Okinawa, Japan.
