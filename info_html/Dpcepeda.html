Short description

Segment 1 consists of a closed cuticular ring, and segments 2 to 11 of one tergal and two sternal plates. Acicular spinesarepresent in dorsal series on segments 1 to 9, butonly the spine on segment 1 is middorsal. From segment 2, the dorsal spines are alternatingly, laterally displaced, to paradorsal positions onsegment 2 and 3, and even more extreme to subdorsal positions on segments 4 to 9.
Long, flexible ventrolateral spines are present on segment 1, and more regular looking acicular spines in lateral series on segments 2 to 9. Lateral tubes are present on segments 2, 5 and 8. Lateroventral glandular cell outletstype 2 are present on segment 10. LTSare present, but LTAS and MTS absent.

Key characters and similar species

are all characterised by the conspicuous, lateral displacement of dorsal spines. In species of Dracoderes, the spines are appear alternatingly in paradorsal positions. This displacement is even more extreme in Dracophyes, where the spines alternatingly would appear in subdorsal positions.

Species of Dracophyesare distinguished by theposition of tubes in the lateralseries. Dracophyes cepedai differs from D. chaac by having lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 (absent in D. chaac), and from D. toyoshioae by lacking tubes on segments 2 to 3, and 6 to 7.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from the Peru Basin in the tropical East Pacific, and livesin muddy sediments at 4152-4160 m depth.
