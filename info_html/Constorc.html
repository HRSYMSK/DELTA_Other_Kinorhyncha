Short description

Species with segment 1 consisting of a closed ring, segments 2 to 10 of one tergal and two sternal plates, and segment 11 of one tergal and one sternal plate. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 10, and lateral series on segments 1 to 9. Cuspidate spines are presentas unpaired ones in middorsal positions on segments 5 and 7, in the lateral series on segments 1, 4, 5, 8 and 9.
MTS and LTAS present.
LTASalways considerably longer than MTS.

Adult dimorphism: Adult dimorphism is likely to occur in all species of Condyloderes. It is still not fully understood for all species, but it appears that in particular occurrenceof acicular spines on segment 10 might differ between stages and sexes, thus researchers should be cautious about relying too much on segment 10 morphology during identification.

Key characters and similar species

All species of Condyloderes are distinguished by the positions of cuspidate spines.
C. storchiis one of only three species with cuspidate spines on the dorsal side (other two are C. shirleyi and C.
agnetis). This specieshas middorsal cuspidate spines on segments 5 and 7, which is shared with C. shirleyi only. However, it can be distinguished from C. shirleyibyits presence of lateral cuspidate spines on segments 1 and 4, and lack of such spines on segments 2, 6 and 7.

Distribution and habitat

gut contents of the shrimp Pleoticus muelleri collected in the San Jorge Gulf in southern Argentina.
