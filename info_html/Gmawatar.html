Gracilideres mawatarii

Gracilideres mawatarii Yamasaki,

Short description

Conspicuously elongate, slender and cylindrical species. The trunk cuticle is extremely thin and flexible, and tegumental plate junctions can be difficult to visualise.
Placids arecompletely reduced, and the neck region forms a closed ring that resemblesan additional segment anterior to the regular trunk segments.
All trunk segments consist of closed, cuticular rings.

Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 3 to 11 ( spines might be alternating slightly to paradorsal positions). A midterminal spine is absent, but the strong middorsal spine on segment 11 could be confused with a midterminal spine. In thelateral series, acicular spines are present on segments 4 to 10, and tubes on segments 1, 2, and 5. LTS and LTASare present.

characters and similar species

Gracilideres mawatariican only be confused with species of Cateriaand Franciscideres. However, the segment composition differs between the three genera, and only G.
mawatariiis the only known kinorhynch with all segments being composed of closed cuticular rings.
In addition, G. mawatarii differs from species of Franciscideresand Cateriaby lacking dorsal spines on segments 1 and 2, but then having dorsal spines on all following segments. The presence of lateral tubes on segments 1 and 2 is furthermore unique for the species.

Distribution and habitat

Thisspecies was described from subtidal, sandy sediments at 55 m depth near Tanegashima Island, Japan. It has not been recorded elsewhere.
