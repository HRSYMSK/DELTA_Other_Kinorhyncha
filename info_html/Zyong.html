Short description

Long, cylindrical specimens, with a very thin cuticle, which makes it difficult to visualise plate junctions and articulations. Segments 1 and 2 consist of closed rings, and segments 3 to 11 of single plates with midventral articulations.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 4, 6, and 8 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2 (very small) and 8.
Cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 5, 8 and 9.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.
MTS always much longer that lateral spines.

Key characters and similar species

Species of Zelinkaderesare usually distinguished from their congeners by the pattern of cuspidate spines. However, the cuspidate spine patterns in Z. klepaliand Z. yongare identical. These two species are distinguished by the presence (in Z. klepali) or absence ( Z. yong) of acicular lateral spines on segment 9.
Z. yongis the only species of Zelinkaderes without lateral acicular spines on segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from the Korean East Sea, close to Pohang on the South Korean west coast, in clean sand 3-6

m depth.
Additional paratypes were collected at Jeju Island, Korea, from intertidal sedimentat Shinchang. However, since the initial collection, the Shinchang area was turned into a public beach park, and the construction work seems to have destroyed the habitat. In search for an alternative collecting site at Jeju Island, the species was found nearby in a beach at Geumneung (33 23 23N 126 14 04 E).
