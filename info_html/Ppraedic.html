Key characters and similar species

Species of Paracentrophyesarebe distinguished by their relatively stout, rectangular trunks, with a first segment composed of a tergalplate, and a broad, sternal platewhich is partly differentiated into episternal and a midsternal plate. This latter trait is unique for the genus. Species of the genus resembles Neocentrophyes and Mixtophyes, but besides the partially differentiated sternal plate of segment 1 that distinguishes it from both genera, Paracentrophyesis distinguished from Neocentrophyesby the presence of lateral terminal spines, and from Mixtophyes by having a less rectangular trunk outline, and much shorter lateral terminal spines (LTS:TL is <32 % for Paracentrophyes, versus > 40% or more for Mixtophyes).

Paracentrophyes lookquite similar, and are most easily distinguished by the distribution pattern of paradorsal and ventrolateral setae.
Paracentrophyes praedictusisdistinguished from other species by the presence of two pairs of ventrolateral setae on segment 1, opposite to only one pair in other species.
Its lateral terminal spines are without the same LTS/TL proportion as P. quadridentatus, but it has longer lateral terminal spines than P. anurus, and shorter than P. sanchezae.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from Carrie Bow Keys in Belize, and has subsequently been collected in Caribbean Panama around Bocas del Toro.
