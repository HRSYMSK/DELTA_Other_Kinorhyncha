Cateria gerlachi

Cateria gerlachi
Higgins, 1968

Short description

elongate, slender and cylindrical species. The trunk cuticle is extremely thin and flexible, and tegumental plate junctions can be difficult to visualise.
Primary spinoscalids are very long, flexible and palp-like. Placids are almost reduced, and appear very indistinct. Segments 1 to 6 are composed of a broad tergal plate, and a narrow sternal plate; the sternal plate gets gradually narrower from segment 1 towards segments 6. Segments 7 to 11 are composed of single tergal plates with midventral articulations.

Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 2 to 4, 6, and 8 to 10 ( males with crenulated spines on segment 10), and in lateral series on segments 6 to 10 ( males with crenulated spines on segment 10). Lateroventral tubes are present on segment 5. Cuspidate spinesare not present.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present; MTS are always much longer than LTS and LTAS.

Key characters and similar species

The two species of Cateria are most easily distinguished by the dorsal organ, present in Cateria styx only. In addition, C. styx has (in addition to LTS and LTAS) short midlateral spines on segment 11. Such spines are also lacking in C. gerlachi.

Distribution and habitat

Thisspecies was described from the east coast of the India, near Visakhapatnam/Waltair, and has been found at several localities in this area. It has in addition been collected at Sri Lanka and the Andaman Islands.
