Triodontoderes anulap

Triodontoderes anulap Sø rensen & Rho,

Short description

Long, cylindrical specimens, with a very thin cuticle, which makes it difficult to visualise plate junctions and articulations. Segments 1 consists of a tergal and a much narrower sternal plates, segments 2 to 4 of one tergaland two sternal plates, and segments 5 to 11 one tergal plate with midventral articulation.
Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11 (segment 1 spines very short), and in lateral series on segments 2 to 10.
Cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 5 to 6, and 8 to 9.
MTS, LTS and LTAS present.
MTS always much longer that lateral spines.

Key characters and similar species

The genus Triodontoderesis easily recognised by their unique segment compositions. The two species, Z. anulap and Z. lagahoo aremost easily distinguished by the presence (in T.
anulap) or absence ( T.
lagahoo) ofcuspidate lateral spines on segment 6.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described from the Chuuk Islands, Micronesia, from sandy sediments at 17 m depth. It has a Tropical West Pacific distribution, and has also been recorded from Saipan.
