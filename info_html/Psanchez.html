Key characters and similar species

Species of Paracentrophyesarebe distinguished by their relatively stout, rectangular trunks, with a first segment composed of a tergal plate, and a broad, sternal plate which is partly differentiated into episternal and a midsternal plate. This latter trait is unique for the genus. Species of the genus resembles Neocentrophyes and Mixtophyes, but besides the partially differentiated sternal plate of segment 1 that distinguishes it from both genera, Paracentrophyesis distinguished from Neocentrophyesby the presence of lateral terminal spines, and from Mixtophyes by having a less rectangular trunk outline, and much shorter lateral terminal spines (LTS:TL is >32 % for Paracentrophyes, versus < 40% or more for Mixtophyes).

Paracentrophyes lookquite similar, and are most easily distinguished by the distribution pattern of paradorsal and ventrolateral setae.
Paracentrophyes sanchezae stands out from all other congeners in three, quite recognisable ways: 1) this is the Paracentrophyes species with the longest lateral terminal spines ( LTS/TL ratio >25), 2) this is the only Paracentrophyes species where females have a middorsal process rather than a spine on segment 10, and 3) this is the on Paracentrophyes species where setae do not appear in continuous series along the segments, and where paradorsal setae are unpaired and missing on most segments.

Distribution and habitat

The specieshas been recorded from the northern part of the Gulf of Mexico, on depths from 54 to 142 m.
