Short description

tergal plate and a small trapezoid sternal plate.
Dorsal anterior segment margin with deep, wedge-shaped incision with narrow, rod-shaped middorsal placid, and ventral anterior margin deep, but broader incision, with elongate, rectangular to triangular midventral placid.
Closing of the head opening follows a lateral contraction, rather than the more common radial closing. Segments 2 to 11 composed of one tergal and two sternal plates.

Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11, in lateral series on segments 3 to 9, and laterodorsally on segment 10.
Cuspidate spines not present. MTS, LTS and LTAS present; LTS about 2/3 of LTAS length.

Key characters and similar species

The species ischaracterised by a segment 1 composed of a tergal plate, and asmall, trapezoid sternal plate – a trait otherwise only shared with Sphenoderes indicus.
The composition of segment 1 and the spine distribution is an interesting mix of Semnoderes and Sphenoderes characters. Thedeep, dorsal wedge-shaped incision on theanterior segment margin of segment 1 resembles the condition in Semnoderes, whereas deep but much broader ventral incision is typical for Sphenoderes. This unique segment 1 composition is unique for Parasemnoderes intermedius.
The distribution of acicular spines follow the pattern found in Sphenoderes, but opposite to both Sphenoderes and Semnoderes, this species does not have cuspidate spines on any segment.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from 5.348 m depth nearthe Kuril-Kamchatka Trench.
