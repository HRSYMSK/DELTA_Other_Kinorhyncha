Short description

Species withconspicuously deepdorsal and ventral concavities along anterior margin of segment 1. Acicular spines present in middorsal series on segments 1 to 11, and in lateral series on segments 2 to 10. Well-developed cuspidate spines present in lateral series on segments 2, 5, 8 and 9 (cuspidate spine on segment 9 more ventral than corresponding acicular spine).
MTS, LTS and LTAS present. LTAS twice as long, and conspicuously stronger than LTS.

Key characters and similar species

The species are distinguished from its congeners A. incomitata and A. oreasby differences in cuspidate spine patterns. It shows closer resemblance with A.
paulaeand A. caeciliae, butis distinguished by itsstrong LTAS, that are twice as long, and more than twice as thick as its LTS.
LTS and LTAS beingnearly equal in length. Opposite to this, in A.
paulaethe LTASand LTS aremore or less equal in length, whereas LTS are longer than LTAS in A. caeciliae.

Distribution and habitat

The specieswas described fromthe Northwest Atlantic, 20 milesoff Fort Pierce, Florida, and is found insandy mud onca. 1 5 0 m depth. It has furthermore been recorded inboth the northern and southern parts of the Gulf of Mexico.
